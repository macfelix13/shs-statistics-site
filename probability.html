Basic Probability Concepts

Probability measures the likelihood of an event occurring:

  • Probability ranges from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain)
  • Use fractions, decimals, or percentages to express probabilities
  • Example: Probability of rolling a 3 on a fair die is 1/6

Compound Events

These involve more than one outcome:

  • Independent events: Two events are independent if the occurrence of one event has no impact on the probability of the other occurring. Examples Flipping a coin and rolling a die: The result of the coin flip doesn’t change the chance of rolling a 6. Choosing a card from one deck and tossing a coin: Drawing a queen doesn’t affect whether the coin lands on heads.
